# Mechanical Properties of AM Alloys

## Static Properties

### Strength (YS, UTS)
- L-PBF Ti64: YS 900-1100 MPa as-built (α'), 800-950 MPa post-HT.
- L-PBF SS316L: YS 500-600 MPa as-built (Hall-Petch + dislocation density).
- L-PBF IN718: YS ~600 MPa as-built, ~1200 MPa after aging (γ''+γ' precipitation).

### Ductility
- As-built Ti64: elongation ~6-8% (limited by α' brittleness).
- Post-HT Ti64: elongation 10-14%.
- Porosity is primary ductility limiter below threshold density (~99.5%).

## Fatigue
- Defects (porosity, lack-of-fusion) act as crack initiation sites.
- Surface roughness degrades HCF (high-cycle fatigue); HIP + machining improves.
- Anisotropy: fatigue life perpendicular to build direction typically lower.

## Fracture Toughness
- AM alloys generally competitive with wrought when defect-free.
- Columnar grain texture can create preferred crack paths along grain boundaries.

## Anisotropy
- Build direction (Z) vs. horizontal (X, Y): differences in YS, ductility, fatigue.
- Typically: horizontal specimens show higher ductility due to grain boundary orientation.
