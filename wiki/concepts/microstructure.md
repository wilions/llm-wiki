# Microstructure in AM Alloys

## Phases and Precipitates

### Titanium (Ti64)
- **α' martensite**: metastable hexagonal phase after rapid L-PBF cooling. 
  Strengthening but brittle. Decomposes to α+β on post-process heat treatment.
- **α+β**: equilibrium dual-phase after HT above β-transus. Ductility improves.

### Nickel Superalloys
- **γ** (FCC matrix) + **γ'** (Ni₃Al, L1₂ ordered) + **γ''** (Ni₃Nb, BCT) precipitates.
- Laves phase (Ni₂Nb) forms in IN718 during solidification; undesirable, dissolves on HT.

### HEA
- Single-phase FCC (Cantor), dual-phase BCC+B2 (AlCoCrFeNi), sigma phase (embrittles).

## Texture
- Strong ⟨001⟩ fiber texture along build direction common in cubic alloys (SS316L, IN718).
- Texture affects elastic anisotropy and fatigue crack initiation.

## Defects
- **Porosity**: keyhole (spherical, high VED), lack-of-fusion (irregular, low VED).
- **Hot cracking**: liquation cracking (grain boundary liquid films), solidification cracking.
- **Residual stress**: tensile on surface, compressive in bulk (L-PBF); relieved by HT.
- **Delamination / warping**: from residual stress + poor support strategy.
